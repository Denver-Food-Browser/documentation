# Updates
A free-to-access regional directory and verification tracker created by and for people who want to find free food and meals in the Denver Metro area.

Features in place
====
- web interface for browsing service listings
- web interface for browsing community verification needs
- sorting & filtering on: service type, day of the week, time of day, venue type, city, zip code, and much more
- verification form with tagging options based on Food Options, Access, Atmosphere, Eligibility Requirements, Other Services, police presence, and more

12/13/25 | By Us, For Us Food Browser update
====
The **user interface** is revealed! üéâ though the database is practically **empty**. The initial data collection process is tedious and time-consuming: we are collecting this data one-by-one for over 200 initial meals and food banks. I've yet to locate a comprehensive, datemarked list so we're making our own.

The current interface is made up of two public-facing parts: the **Food Browser** and the **Verification Tracker**.
One of the core capabilities and most requested features of this directory must be a **verification and auditing** system for maintaining service data over time. At first this will rely on **local volunteer** effort. If the directory proves useful and effective, verifications will be encouraged by any and all service goers.
    
This is the current pipeline for service listings:
1. Service information is added from websites, recent fliers, word of mouth, etc. (online verificaton)
2. Service is listed in the public-facing Verification tracker.
3. Community members visit a listed service location during listed service hours and submit an online Verification form about their experience. (live verification)
4. Services are re-added to the Verification tracker whenever their listings grow stale (no updates or verifications within a to-be-determined time period).
